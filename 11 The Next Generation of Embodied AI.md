Smart Line Robotics introduces the world's first modular scissor-structure embodied robot.
For years, the robotics industry has focused almost exclusively on one question:
How do we make robots smarter?
At Smart Line Robotics, we asked a different question:
What if the robot's body itself is wrong?
Artificial Intelligence is advancing at an astonishing pace.
Large language models.
Vision.
Reasoning.
Planning.
Agentic AI.
Yet most embodied robots are still built upon a mechanical architecture invented more than half a century ago.
Heavy rotary joints.
Large reduction gearboxes.
Multiple oversized motors fighting gravity every second.
The intelligence has evolved.
The body has not.

Physical AI Needs a Physical Architecture
We believe the next revolution of robotics is not another AI model.
It is a new physical architecture.
Our newly developed embodied robot replaces traditional humanoid legs with a modular scissor lifting structure and combines it with our proprietary nonlinear scissor robotic arm.
This creates a robot designed specifically for real industrial work—not for mimicking human anatomy.
Instead of asking a robot to imitate people,
we ask:
What is the most efficient body for the task?
That question changes everything.

Current version:
•	Height: 0.7 m – 2.8 m (2.3–9.2 ft) 
•	Expandable to 3.6 m (12 ft) through modular leg sections 
•	Working height exceeds 3.3 m (11 ft) 
Because the legs are modular, customers simply add or remove scissor modules according to their application.
Warehouses.

Aircraft maintenance.
High-bay logistics.
One robot body adapts to many environments.

Energy Efficiency Designed into the Structure
Most humanoid robots continuously consume energy simply to remain standing.
Every motor must constantly generate torque to support the robot's own weight.
Our design works differently.
The dual electric screw actuators inside the scissor legs become mechanically self-locking after positioning.
Once stopped,
they consume essentially no holding power.
Meanwhile, our nonlinear scissor arm performs most operations through horizontal extension and retraction, dramatically reducing gravitational loading.
Instead of constantly fighting gravity,
the robot lets geometry do the work.
The result is approximately
12× lower overall energy consumption
than conventional humanoid architectures.
This is not merely better control software.
It is structural intelligence.
________________________________________
Designed for Logistics
Our nonlinear scissor arm has already demonstrated reach lengths approaching 3 meters.
For warehouse picking, only about one meter of horizontal reach is required to access most shelves.
That means our robot can operate with much wider shelving layouts.
Benefits include:
•	Higher storage density 
•	Fewer warehouse aisles 
•	Increased picking efficiency 
•	Lower infrastructure cost 
Future versions will automatically deploy stabilizing outriggers whenever wider reach is required.
________________________________________
Simple Teaching Instead of Complex Programming
Programming robots remains one of the largest barriers to automation.
Our robot uses a demonstration-based workflow.
An operator simply guides the robot's end effector through the desired trajectory once.
The robot records the motion and reproduces it automatically.
Applications include:
•	Grinding 
•	Welding 
•	Machine tending 
•	Logistics 
•	Pick-and-place 
•	Assembly 
For simple operations, even a smartphone interface can control the robot directly.
Automation becomes accessible without robotics specialists.
